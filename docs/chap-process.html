MASTER THESIS HANDBOOK TEXTILE MANAGEMENT – 15 & 30 CREDITS

2 PROCESS OF THESIS WRITING & DEFENCE

2.1 Introductory meeting and information meeting

In the period before the start of the course, the first introductory meeting regarding the thesis process will be held by the thesis course coordinator. The purpose of the meeting is to initiate and inform about the thesis writing process. The focus is on describing the thesis writing process, the structure of the course, inform about important documents and deadlines. There will be an additional information meeting at the commencement of the course, usually in mid-January for 2 year students while in early April for 1 year students. In this information meeting you will receive more specific information regarding the course and important deadlines. Along with information related to finalization of the course will also be provided.

It is very important to attend these meetings organized since this is your opportunity to ask questions about the thesis writing process. The opportunity to ask questions to the course coordinator beyond these meetings is limited and you will be directed to documents and fellow students.

2.2 Getting started

2.2.1 Group size

The thesis is written in groups of two students. The normal procedure is that you form a group of two students that share the same overall interest in a topic (could be as broad as “supply chain management” or “marketing”).

Based on extensive experience in tutoring theses it is our firm belief that this is an appropriate size. Apart from being an added resource in regards to working hours it seems that the possibility to continuously discuss the theses with a co-author sparks creativity and energizes the thesis writing process. In addition, many students find it relieving to share responsibility and handle critique at the final seminar when they are not alone.

You should actively search for a co-author. If you do not have a co-author in mind you can contact other students through the program portal on Canvas. If you have not found one by the time you register for thesis writing, the course coordinator may pair you up with other students with similar interest. You are then to contact these students to discuss if you could find an interest in common. Only in special cases do we allow students to write individually.

Thesis groups having more than two students is not allowed.

2.2.2 First thing – get registered

In order to get a tutor assigned to you, course coordinators needs to know your thesis topic and with whom you will write the thesis. Thus the thesis writing process begins with registering for supervision. This you must do via the program portal on Canvas under Two-year/One-year master thesis. Note that all information needs to be filled in order to have a valid registration. Only those with a valid registration will be appointed a tutor.

Updated deadlines for registration via program portal on Canvas are found in Kronox and Canvas.

Following registration you will receive access to the course homepage on Canvas which will contain details of how to proceed. The entry requirements for thesis projects are stated later in this manual in Chapter 3 and also in the course syllabus.

2.2.3 Thesis topic

Ultimately the thesis project validates the quality of the programme. This means that students who master the skills acquired before and during the programme to a significant degree, will able to produce a thesis project that touches the science frontier in a specific textile management domain. A student who is able to synthesize their knowledge, understanding, skills, abilities and judgement during the thesis course can be called a Master of Textile Management. Complete learning objectives are stated in the study programme syllabus. The project should address the scientific aspects of a professional textile management issue or problem. In order to assure proper scientific level, the project is preferably conducted in the proximity of research areas where the Swedish School of Textiles is active. Alternatively, enterprises, institutes or other academic organizations with research focus can host the project and offer topics for thesis writing. Companies and organizations may also offer topics for thesis writing. In the case that students choose to cooperate with a company, it is their responsibility to make sure to maintain the scientific level of the thesis so that it does not become a consultancy report. Make sure to continuously discuss this with your assigned tutor. One possibility is to write the thesis and then offer the company a presentation especially for them (or invite them to the presentation at the graduation event) and focus on the managerial twist then. Thesis writing may not be combined with an internship. You are expected to spend the full semester (i.e. 20 weeks/40 hours per week) on developing the master thesis, or one quarter (i.e. 10 weeks/40 hours per week) for a magister thesis.

Another important point to note is that the thesis group are themselves responsible for finding a topic/theme for the thesis. To assure that the student manages the project, it is mandatory that students take the initiative to develop and articulate a topic. It can be done through browsing through topics suggested on Canvas, discussing with your teachers, or be based on students’ own interests. If you find a topic suggested on Canvas interesting, contact the one that is responsible for that topic directly before you start writing. The thesis coordinator will not keep a stock of ready-made research questions for the students.

2.2.4 Developing a thesis proposal and its submission

During the thesis registration process via Canvas you will have the possibility to submit a one page thesis proposal. The proposal should contain a description of the problem area within a specific field of research that you are interested in. This proposal will be used for designating a tutor to you. You cannot choose tutor, but when you register for thesis via Canvas you may suggest two preferences of your choice (for registration form, see Appendix section A). The thesis proposal should contain, in addition to the problem area and field of research, also an indication of what conceptual lens you are planning to use, empirical material that is expected to be collected and how (method). If the proposal has been developed in relation to a company, researcher or organization, this should be clearly specified in the proposal document. A proposal that is not sufficient will be handed back for revision. Revision or late hand in may delay the appointment of tutors. Deadline for handing the proposal is found in Canvas and Kronox. The thesis proposal template is available on the program portal on Canvas.

The next step in the process is the information meeting (already specified in section 2.1).

2.3 Supervision/Tutoring

Tutors/Supervisors will be appointed by course coordinators based on a match between the tutor’s competences and the chosen problem area of the students, based on the proposal for thesis topic. The course responsible will inform the students about which tutor they have been appointed through Canvas at the start of the course.

Following this, you will be expected to be in contact with your assigned tutor by email, arranging for the first meeting in your seminar group (the seminar group generally consists of 2-4 theses that the tutor is supervising). You should be in contact within 7 days from the Information meeting. If not, please contact the course coordinator. From here on your main contact is the assigned tutor. Authors are advised to use the time leading up to the first seminar for literature studies and to begin crafting their introductory chapter.

Master and Magister theses are generally tutored in two ways. One, via a series of mandatory group seminars (about four and three respectively). In addition to these group seminars, the thesis group can also obtain individual tutoring on their work, if they wish to. This has to be arranged per group with the tutor, and be sure to prepare yourself carefully for such meetings. The authors may also seek advice and tutoring from other teachers when it comes to specific issues but this is not to be taken for granted. It is good to inform your tutor of such additional contacts. Matters that could easily be solved by e.g. consulting the literature should not be brought forward to the tutor/other teachers. AND, do not forget that there are probably people in your network that you can use as external readers in the process … you will always benefit from one more reader!!

But what is tutoring?

Tutoring is guidance only. You are solely responsible for your thesis and should be able to explain and defend all choices you have made in the process. The tutor is there to assist/guide you in the process of making these choices.

Tutors are only available during the course and will not give extra supervision after the course has ended. In case of requirement of additional tutoring, a formal requisition has to be made by the student group, which would then be up for consideration by the Director of Studies in Textile Management. In some cases, only a few additional hours can be allocated, but only during the next thesis course period.

The following example shows a possible structure of group seminars. Changes may occur, for example, an additional seminar may be added, etc. All details and changes need to be planned in advance, in conversation with the tutor.

Seminar 1

The students and the tutor meet for the first time and get to know each other. During this meeting the chosen topic and expectations on the role of the tutor and thesis group respectively should be discussed and clarified. Each thesis group has prepared a document based on their preliminary idea of topic (the thesis proposal). The thesis topic should be approximately delimited. The tutor gives feedback on the idea.

Depending on the status of the preliminary idea there may be more contacts with the tutor before we enter the stage of seminar 2.

Additionally, this seminar is used to set a timeline for all remaining seminars, including the deadline for circulating drafts among the seminar group members before each seminar. The tutor decides if the submission is made via dedicated pages on Canvas or via email. After the first seminar, each group is responsible for updating the “Supervision Seminar Progress” page located under the course on Canvas. This update must include:

  1. The timeline for all seminars, entered under the column “Planned date and time for the supervision seminars”.

  2. Progress updates for each seminars, entered under the column “Actual date and time for the supervision seminars”, along with explanations in the “Comment” column for any deviations from the planned timeline.

The above updates must be made within the specified deadline mentioned on the above-mentioned page on Canvas.

Students must ensure that a complete schedule for all seminars is finalized with the tutor during Seminar 1. If your assigned tutor has not contacted you by the course start date, you are responsible for reaching out to them to introduce yourself and to arrange Seminar 1. If you still receive no response within one week after the course starts, you must inform the course coordinator.

If a prescheduled group seminar cannot take place due to the tutor’s uninformed unavailability, you must first contact the tutor to arrange a new meeting time. If the tutor does not respond within one week, you must notify the course coordinator to prevent further delays in supervision.

Each student group is responsible for updating the “Supervision Seminar Progress” page on Canvas. The deadline for these updates is provided directly on the same page.

Seminar 2

The introductory chapter including background, problem discussion and purpose is completed and discussed. The authors also present the first version of their literature study and their ideas concerning methodology, disposition and time planning. Each group has prepared a working thesis document that is submitted to the tutor and the assigned opponents in advance. The opponents are to submit a detailed opposition to the tutor when the seminar begins. The tutors will inform you about what thesis you should read and discuss, in advance.

In seminar two and in the following seminars, students are to practice three roles.

  • The role as author – able to constructively discuss pros and cons in your submitted manuscript.

  • The role as opponent – able to identify advantages and shortcomings in a submitted manuscript, and to discuss these in a constructive manner.

  • And finally, the role as an active observer – able to listen and reflect on manuscripts where you are not involved either as author or opponent.

The seminars are excellent opportunities to train in these roles, as well as being part of the examination of the course.

Seminar 3

For magister theses as the timeframe is shorter, Seminar 3 and Seminar 4 can be merged into one. This is decided by the tutor in discussion with his/her thesis groups in the Seminar 1.

Discussion of the ongoing work with a further focus on frame of reference and methodology. Again, each group has to prepare a working thesis document that is submitted to the tutor and the assigned opponents in advance. It should contain a re-worked version of problem, purpose and research questions. It should also describe the theoretical framework and include a proposal for the method of empirical field work, containing e.g. sampling method, survey, interview guide, experimental design or the like. The authors should also present ideas of how to analyse their data. The document is submitted in advance. Assigned opponents are to submit a detailed opposition when the seminar begins.

Seminar 4

The almost finished manuscript is presented. The result and analysis work should be finalized, along with the discussion and conclusion sections, although this is not the finally edited version. The manuscript is submitted in advance. The opponents are expected to comprehensively analyse the entire manuscript in order to contribute to improvements before the final seminar. Assigned opponents are to submit a detailed opposition at a time decided by the tutor.

2.4 Upload Final thesis for opposition/defence

When the final version of your manuscript is ready to defend you need to upload a PDF version of the manuscript on Canvas. There is a general deadline for all theses (see Canvas/Kronox). You should communicate with your tutor (to seek informal permission) before your submit. From experience, authors who insist on defending their thesis against the advice of their tutor run a high risk of failing.

You will be informed about the presentation/defence schedule, who is your primary grader, opponent(s) and whom you are to oppose on in due time via Canvas course page. This information is provided usually within 4 days after the deadline of manuscript submission. When the defence-related information is published/communicated, all submitted manuscripts are uploaded on Canvas. As an opponent, you must download the thesis manuscript of the authors who you are opposing from Canvas. Additional information about this process will be provided on Canvas or by email by the course coordinator.

2.5 The final seminar

The final seminar for each group consists of 2 components. 1. A defence of own thesis. 2. Acting as the main opponent of 1 assigned thesis group.

2.5.1 The disposition of the defence

The final seminar is chaired by the primary grader of the thesis. It is about 45-60 minutes long per group, with the following approximate distribution of the allotted time:

  • Presentation of the thesis by the authors, 10 min.

  • Opponents presents thesis. 5-10 minutes

  • Opponent-author dialogue, led by the opponent, 20 min.

  • Questions and comments from the audience, 10 min.

  • Primary grader´s questions 5-10 min.

The primary grader can suggest some minor changes to the seminar format.

The final seminars are usually held during the final weeks of each term (according to the academic schedule). We expect all thesis authors to be available during the examination weeks. For exact dates and times, check on the Thesis course activity on Kronox and Canvas.

Thesis authors cannot choose the dates for their final seminar and their opposition.

Authors who have not finished their thesis by then will be given the opportunity to present it on a later occasion, usually 3 months later (end of August).

As a piece of advice!

The authors must prepare for critique and think through their defence before the final seminar by going through their strengths and weaknesses that they wish to bring up. It is recommended that you let someone read and review the draft before the seminar. A well prepared defence is characterized by the authors showing that they have thought through their choices and that they are well aware of the weaknesses, but have handled them the best they could. It should show that these choices have been made by the authors based on a logical arguments and not because they “were advised to do so”. It is also wise that the authors, in the event that the opponents find a weakness they have not prepared for, rather admit that the opponents have a point and engage in an open discussion regarding the issue, rather than acting defensively or providing justifications based on weak arguments.

2.5.2 The opposition

The opponents are responsible for ascertaining that the purposes of the seminar are fulfilled, by means of a dialogue with the author. These purposes are as follows:

  • that the author receives in-depth feedback, in addition to that given by the primary grader, and

  • that the seminar becomes an additional learning ground for the author, the audience and the opponent in regards to the art of evaluating works of research.

The opponents are responsible for keeping the dialogue with the authors at an academic level and deliver constructive criticism. This means that strengths and weaknesses should be discussed, and that the opponent should be prepared to suggest alternative approaches, perspectives, interpretations, etc. It also means that the criticism should be presented in a dialogue with the author.

This often has to do with how the different parts of the thesis are connected to each other. To get the grade passed the opposition can follow an example of the outline presented below:

Before the seminar begins the opponents should hand over their individual oppositions to the primary grader. The structure of these written documents could also be used for the oral opposition at the defence. The written opposition is an individual examination. Therefore each individual in the student group has to write a unique opposition, using the following structure.

Formulate adequate scientific questions for the defending student group.

  • These questions can be related to an overall view (e.g. does it all fit together, how do purpose and theory fit together, how purpose-method fit together, how do theory-method fit together).

  • Motivate and argue why each one of these questions are relevant. Why is this question important to ask?

  • Describe an alternative solution on how to address the question. How could the authors have done differently? What are the advantages/disadvantages when comparing solutions?

  • Discuss other points related to the thesis and the different parts (both major and minor comments can be brought up).

In chapter three, the objectives and evaluation criteria (in terms of the learning outcomes) used in the course is presented. These criteria form a good starting point for the formulation of the written opposition. You may work together on the task, but the hand in must be unique for each individual.

However, a fundamental issue is that the opponents should not go through the thesis page by page or even chapter-wise. Instead, most of the allotted time should be devoted to discussing the thesis as a whole and to the specifics which, according to the opponent are critically important in an evaluation of the thesis as a whole.

The opponents should at all cost avoid the following:

  • Be overly positive in a general term – it gives the impression that they have not made an effort or have not succeeded in understanding the thesis intrinsically.

  • “Tear down” the thesis. Instead they should suggest reasonable alternatives. There is no sense in suggesting a completely different methodology at this point in time – though it might be valuable to discuss what could have been done as a part of understanding the limitations of the present study. Don’t go through the thesis page by page.

  • Mix large and small issues. This gives the impression that the opponents are unable to distinguish details from in-depth issues.

  • Not allowing the authors to defend, or being unable/unwilling to listen and respond to the arguments presented by the authors.

  • Devote too much time to formalia. Issues related to language or design should be part of the written opposition but should not be the foundation of a discussion.

2.5.3 After defence! Grading and final steps

After the defence, students are only permitted to make minor changes to the thesis, i.e. in formalia, that includes correcting spelling mistakes, minor errors in the reference list, before the final document is uploaded to the university webpage, after recieving a passing grade. It is important that the thesis remains consistent in form and content from the version graded by the graders/examiners. Nonetheless, the students must prepare a response letter answering to the concerns raised by the opponents. Students can refer to the response template available on Canvas or in Appendix section A.

When you have defended your thesis, the following processes will happen:

  • The thesis will be checked by Urkund for plagiarism (you find more information about Urkund in the link above)

  • The report from Urkund will be sent to the graders who will decide if there is cause to suspect plagiarism

Before receiving a grade, students need to upload the following documents via Canvas:

  • Final Seminar opposition document.

  • Self-declared thesis process diary. (described in Section 2.5.4)

  • Scientific poster in PDF format. (described in Section 2.5.7)

  • Response to the opponent comments.

The self-declaration effort agrement should be submitted separately. It should not be included in the thesis report.

In addition, the students need to submit the GDPR form, if any. Please read Section 5.3 for more information.

You can only make minor/cosmetic changes to the thesis manuscript after the final seminar presentation/defence. Therefore, it is not permitted to submit an incomplete thesis for final seminars and grading.

Upon receiving a passing grade, students must upload the final document in accordance with the specified process:

Deadline for submission of final thesis documents is found on Canvas and Kronox. After the final grades are received, there will be a list of thesis numbers published on Canvas that must be added to the front page. Not until you receive the thesis number will you be able to upload the thesis in PDF format on the webpage of University of Borås. You upload/submit the thesis on the following link:

https://www.hb.se/en/student/services/submission-of-thesis/

We have the ambition that all theses at the Swedish School of Textiles and Univeristy of Borås should be archived in DiVA (a digital archive for academic texts from universities in Sweden). When you publish your thesis in DiVA it will increase access and visibility for your thesis. It will also make it searchable on Google, as well as in the Swedish databases for theses (Uppsök and Uppsatser.se).

To be able to publish your thesis on DiVA, we need your consent. You give your consent when you upload your thesis. The consent is individual which means that all authors of a thesis must upload together.

NOTE! If, for some reason, you strongly object to the publication of the thesis digitally, you refrain from checking the box when you submit. For more information, follow this link:

https://www.hb.se/en/university-library/academic-language/publish-student-thesis/

If you wish to have a printed version of you thesis you will have to organize this on your own and cover all expenses.

2.5.4 Thesis process diary

The diary is a self-declaration made individually by the thesis group students to communicate the individual contribution to the thesis both in terms of the process (participation in meetings, total time spent on the thesis) and the final written thesis (e.g. parts that you have written yourself, responsible for formatting etc) as well as use of AI technologies. To the extent that there have been a clear division of work, this should be evident by the documentation.

The template for the diary and instructions will be available on the thesis course page on Canvas. You are free to add additional details as you feel necessary to reflect your division of work for the thesis.

In the event that you feel that you are unable to make any distinction in work division or the same details apply, you may either use some of the same text – but all students must be individually responsible for their own correct documentation. This is a formal document, which may be used to make a distinction between grades or to be addressed in the case of any issues with the thesis. An approved version of the is required with the other parts of thesis submission for grading (as mentioned in Section 2.5.3)

2.5.5 Feedback and Grade

The course consists of the following elements for grading:

  • Active participation during the thesis course (including group seminars and submission for midway check-in).

  • A written thesis.

  • A final seminar that consists of defence of thesis and opposition of another group’s thesis.

  • An individual diary of the thesis writing process.

  • A poster to be showcased at the graduation event (EXIT).

  • A response letter to opponent comments.

Roughly within three weeks after your final defence, you will receive an email with grade sheet that consists of the final grade of the thesis course and a grading motivation jointly made by the examination committee. This email will be sent by the education administrator on the behalf of examiner. There will be no pre-decision discussions while you wait for the grade.

The grade is based on the ECTS scale with the grades A, B, C, D, E, F, and Fx. All elements of the course must receive at least the grade E or G in order pass. Only if the thesis is graded Fx (in need of revision), can the authors make more extensive changes in accordance with the comments made in the grading motivation.

The comments may alter from the comments in the seminar and it is up to the thesis group to interpret the changes needed for the thesis to pass, but you may do any changes you wish during this time. A clear point-by-point response to comments made on the thesis should be enclosed by the authors during thesis resubmission. The students can use the response template available at the course page on Canvas (also available in the Appendix section A ).

There are three opportunities for submitting your thesis within a year (June, August and January) and dates will be available on Canvas and Kronox. You do not have to register for the course in these cases. You submit your thesis via the same link that you find under submission of thesis above. If you receive the grade Fx twice you may apply to change primary grader. This is done using the form that is available by the Student center (see link below).

https://www.hb.se/en/student/services/forms/

In total you have five opportunities to get the thesis examined. Should you receive the grade Fx the fifth time, you receive the grade F.

There may be a scenario that a thesis is awarded a passing grade by the examiner/grader and the authors are not satisfied with the evaluation and want the reassessment of the grade. In such a case, the authors/students are entitled to request a reassessment of the examination. Requests should contain a detailed written account for the reasons. Students must use the form 9 - Request for reassessment of examination (pdf)available on this link to make a formal request for reassessment. It is recommended that the request is made within three weeks of the notification of the grade.

Please refer to the reassessment form for more details on rules and procedure for reassessment request.

2.5.6 If the grade is not registered in Ladok on time

All elements of the course must be considered passed before the grade is registered in Ladok and in order for them to be considered this the following must be fulfilled:

  • The thesis have been uploaded on time in line with instructions

  • The thesis number is placed on the front page of the thesis

  • The thesis have been analyzed by Urkund and an assessment of the result has been made by the graders with no plagiarism found

  • Individual diary must be submitted

  • Examiner has handed in the grade report to the administration

In case of delay, the students can contact the administrator at a1@hb.se

2.5.7 The graduation event EXIT and poster

During the first week in June there is a graduation event at the Swedish School of Textiles called EXIT that will take place in the Textile Fashion Center. The date for this event is found on Canvas and Kronox. This ceremony involves all graduate students at the Swedish School of Textiles including students of Textile engineering and Textile and Fashion Design (that will organize a cat walk) and is open for public.

Participation in EXIT is compulsory in order to receive a total grade on the course via display of a poster related to thesis. This is done in order to meet the learning outcomes that relate to communication with different groups and market advanced level of knowledge in textile management by making the findings of your research project accessible to the textile industry and other interested parties. We strongly believe your scientific presentation will benefit from this activity.

The students can also invite themselves company contacts, friends and family on this day. More information regarding the agenda for the day will be available in due time by the EXIT organizing committee.

Poster Exhibition

As a part of the graduation event each thesis group will prepare a poster that will be on display at the Textile Fashion Center. The purpose of the posters is to evoke interest for your thesis and its result. It is also a way for you to present yourself to the visitors at the event. Apart from purpose, result and managerial conclusions you must thus focus on catching the interest of the public. You find the logo for the Swedish School of Textiles on the University webpage (see link below). Choose the version “Grundutförande”, “Svart logotype” (black) and Jpg format.

http://www.hb.se/Genvagar/Press-och-media/Logotyper/The-Swedish-School-of-Textiles/

Important to consider when creating the poster:

  • Use a clear message/title

  • Use pictures but remember to add reference if it is not your own

  • You may use color print

  • Avoid large paragraphs of text, bullet points create a more accessible impression and are easier to read. Text paragraphs may be used but to save space

  • Be frugal about details, use less than in ordinary text

  • Positioning – think about how you use the space on your poster. What is the main message? What is supporting arguments?

  • The purpose of the study? Why did you do it? What was your main conclusion? Based on what material do you make the conclusions? What are the most significant contributions? How may the results contribute to the textile industry?

  • Keep methodology brief

  • Present your results in the most attractive way possible. Use figures (not too complex), pictures, tables and colors. Sometimes it might be easier to guide the reader by using text to the pictures and tables. When using multiple figures, don’t put them too close. Give space to each part.

  • Tables – consider what do you want to communicate with the table? Use 3D? Colors?

  • The findings and managerial implications are important. Give them a logical position so that they are emphasized. Refer back to the thesis for further information.

  • References – limit the number of references, use smaller size for the text, choose one font and stick it.

  • Choose font freely for the text on the poster but no more than two different.

  • Remember that the poster should give an integrated and uniform appearance.

  • Be brief, clear and consistent

  • Keep your audience in mind when writing the poster. The purpose is to catch their interest and make them interested in learning more – from you or by reading your thesis.

The exact poster format and mode for submission will be notified about four weeks prior to the EXIT event. The poster should be designed in the poster format. Make sure to align the measure of the paper before you start designing your poster to avoid blurred pictures, figures, and tables.

The university will handle the printing of the scientific poster. Before submitting, carefully review your poster to ensure its quality. The poster submission deadline is usually one week before EXIT. For the exact deadline, please refer to Canvas. The course coordinator will collect the posters and ensure they are displayed on time for EXIT.
