5 IMPORTANT ISSUES

5.1 Ethical issues

All theses produced at universities are by law public documents that have to be made available to all. If, for any reason, you need to disguise information about e.g. companies or respondents, you may make them anonymous. It is the authors who are responsible for the process of making information anonymous. If, for some reason, it is not enough to make key elements anonymous it is possible to make the thesis “confidential” for a limited time of two years. This means that the final thesis will be put in an archive for two years before it is published as the rest of the theses. To make theses confidential involves a lengthy process that involves legal aid and is an exception. Not also that even if the thesis is made confidential the opponents must have the full document so that it is discussed in a closed seminar with only opponents and examiner present. If authors wish to work with data that cannot be made public, they should immediately consult their tutor. Try to avoid putting yourselves in the position where the company demands too much of anonymity. Better to choose another topic or company.

The issue of anonymity is closely related to ethics in the thesis. It is customary to ask respondents if the material may be used as it is or if they wish conceal any part of it up. Firms are often willing to share with thesis authors such information that they would normally not want to share with their competitors or with the public. Unfortunately, there are the rare cases when thesis authors have abused this fact for personal gain. This should be strictly avoided. Conversely, a firm or organisation that financially supports the thesis work may try to abuse the authors’ ability to obtain information from competitors. The authors should refrain from being used in such a way and consult the tutor if in doubt.

There are of course other ethical aspects to consider when writing a thesis. Company representatives that will be interviewed should for example be informed if a company or external organization has commissioned the thesis. Consult literature on methodology regarding the ethical aspects of the thesis and be sure to bring it up during supervision.

The thesis authors have copyright according to Swedish copyright law. The fact that the thesis is used for obtaining academic credits, however, imposes certain restrictions in their exclusive right to the manuscript.

5.2 Critique to sources and plagiarism

When you write the thesis it is important to think about the credibility and quality to all sources – oral and written – that you use as material for the thesis. It is also important that you clearly show what sources you have used and how you have used them or there is a risk that it will be interpreted as plagiarism. For more information regarding this and scientific writing in general, you may visit the library webpage:

https://www.hb.se/en/university-library/writing-and-referencing/

5.3 GDPR

Students may only process personal data in scope of their education in accordance with these rules and the responsible teachers’ instructions.

Personal data means any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual where an identifiable person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identification number (e.g. social security number) or one or more factors specific to his physical, physiological, mental, economic, cultural or social identity (e.g. name and first name, date of birth, biometrics data, fingerprints, DNA…)

Undergraduate and Master level students (first-cycle and second-cycle) may never process so called special categories of personal data (integritetskänsliga personuppgifter). This include any data that may reveal racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, union membership, health data, sexual life and sexual orientation, as well as the processing of genetic data, biometric data, privileged information (sekretessbelagda uppgifter), data about transgressions of the law (lagöverträdelser), sensitive economic data, and descriptions and valuations of personal qualities and circumstances, or circumstances that are otherwise close to the private sphere.

In case it is deemed necessary to process personal data, the processing shall be done on the basis of consent. Students’ processing of personal data thus require that the person whose personal data is desired to process consent to the processing.

Consent to process personal data must be obtained in writing before the processing begins using the university’s templates. The consent should include a description of, inter alia, the purpose of the processing and what personal data is necessary to collect in order to achieve that purpose. Therefore, consider these questions carefully and clearly explain what personal data that will be processed and for what purpose, so that the person whose personal data is to be processed understands what the processing means. Contact course responsible or supervisor for the consent template or if you have any questions about how to fill in the template.

Once the consent has been obtained, personal data may be processed according to the consent. The consent thus sets the boundaries for what kind of processing is permitted. If other processing is required, a new consent must be obtained before the processing begins. In case a consent is withdrawn, the processing must end. A with-drawn consent does not however affect any processing that already has taken place.

Information containing personal data that has been collected must be stored on the students’ home directory in the university’s IT environment (“F:”) or on the students’ Google Drive provided by the university (G Suite for Education). Personal data may also be stored temporarily on mobile devices or removable storage media if necessary.

The personal data that has been collected should be erased when it’s no longer necessary for the purpose of the processing, but no later than when the assignment has been passed.

Violation of these rules may be subject to disciplinary actions (warning or suspension) according to law.

The physical copies of originally signed consent forms should be submitted to the course responsible immediately after the final thesis is submitted for grading. It is the responsibility of the student(s)/thesis author(s) to make sure that the consent forms are duly signed and submitted on time.

The university has a GDPR consent form template (available in the appendix section) that must be filled appropriatly and used for taking consent from respondents to process personal data.

5.4 Financing

The main rule is that the student themselves are responsible for financing the thesis work, including costs for data collection and printing. If external sources of finance are opted for, it may be a good idea to approach local, regional or national authorities, or organisations such as unions, The Chamber of Commerce, The Swedish Employers’ Confederation and suchlike, rather than individual firms. This is because a master thesis should treat a problem of general interest rather than solving a specific party’s problem.

If the authors wish to write a thesis on a problem that falls within the domain of a current research program in Textile Management, the school itself may be willing to cover direct costs. Such decisions are taken by the person responsible for the research programme in question.
